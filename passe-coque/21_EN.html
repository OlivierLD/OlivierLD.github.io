The association
"The boat is not an end, it is a means..."
The Passe‑Coque association's mission is to save and restore abandoned boats or boats destined for deconstruction, to pass them on to associations, sailing schools or NGOs which will promote access to sailing to the greatest number through ecological, social, sporting, cultural, humanitarian and solidarity projects.

The genesis...

Passe‑Coque was born in 2019 from the friendship and experience of Bernard de Ravignan and Pierre-Jean Jannin, then administrator and president of Sail The World (STW). The idea was simple: create a dynamic of rehabilitation of sailboats from the 70s.

« “As a maritime expert, I had the opportunity to refit a big boat (60 feet Mauric) and found that the project carried out by Passe‑Coque was an achievable challenge. »
- PJ Jannin.

...From dream to reality...

Under the complicit gaze of Bernard, then involved in the La Cardinale adventure, Pierre-Jean took the helm and launched the first refits. At the end of 2022, more than 25 boats have been saved from deconstruction for as many supported projects, the adventure is launched!

“Passe‑Coque is above all a human and collective adventure.
The boats in our fleet are not simple hulls saved from deconstruction or abandonment but a real maritime heritage revitalized for useful navigation. Savory adventures that we wish to bring to the members of our association, volunteers, patrons, through the new projects that will be entrusted to them”.
- PJ Jannin.

...New projects

In an ecological and social justice approach, Passe‑Coque is launching the first Associative Boat Club in 2024. The idea is to allow as many people as possible to navigate usefully, according to their income and to create social bonds by participating in participatory projects.

Other boat transformation projects are being studied: passenger transport, upcycling, training or the organization of events such as the Passe‑Coque Trophy (a 4L trophy of the sea)...

“Echoing the Transquadra, we want to launch a nautical rally for young people on old boats... revitalized of course! »
- PJ Jannin

...The referents...

For each boat transmitted, Passe‑Coque appoints a volunteer representative in charge of coordinating the boat refit work and liaising with our partner associations.

The referent has a central role in our organization!

He is a true master builder of boat revitalization. Both jack of all trades and committed, our representatives proudly uphold the values of our association.

The nautical Eco-Village
"Dream of another world..."
The nautical Eco-village project is about dreaming bigger. It's dreaming of a place that questions our needs as boaters, a place to write new stories around our passion for boating.

Our ambition...

Passe‑Coque wishes to bring all its expertise to a major project by purchasing a shipyard allowing new activities to come to life.

A Collective Interest Cooperative Society is thus being created around an ambitious collective aiming to develop a virtuous nautical ecosystem around varied activities such as: refit, upcycling, training or Low -Tech.

An integration project will also be set up in order to respond to a societal challenge of returning to employment in professions in shortage.

...The activities

Our collective organized under shared governance will offer a varied audience the opportunity to re-enchant our world. We will offer:

  • Committed, economical and sober cruises to reconnect with life.
  • Training to learn how to scull, rediscover the sky with the basics of celestial navigation or dead reckoning.
  • Low-Tech workshops to design your new navigation aid tools, simple, economical and energy-efficient.
  • Manual workshops to sew your sails or learn the basics of seamanship, mechanics, etc.
  • To create links around festive events.
