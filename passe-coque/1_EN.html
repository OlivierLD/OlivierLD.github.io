The Passe-Coque association, created in 2019 in the Morbihan department, sticks to the simple idea of saving and refitting abandonned or slated-for-demolition boats, to then transmit them to associations, sailing schools or NGOs where they'll facilitate access to sailing for a great number of people, usefully navigating through ecological, social, humanitarian and united projects.

1.n. The fine art of getting wet and becoming ill while slowly going nowhere at great expense.

The Passe-Coque ecosystem - regeneration of sailboats

The projects

The true reason for being of Passe-Coque, since 2019 we have been supporting project leaders engaged in the social, societal and ecological transition of our world. Our team's mission is to draw links between boats without projects and projects without boats.

The shipyard

Passe-Coque's know-how is demonstrated on a daily basis in our premises in Saint Philibert where our boats come back to life under the supervision of our experts. Our 350 m2 workshop allows us to carry out the refit of 5 sailboats simultaneously.

The Passe-Coque Club

Our associative boat club reflects the soul of our action: sharing a fleet of sailboats with as many people as possible. Our packages adapt to your income in order to give all adventure lovers the opportunity to experience unique moments.

The Passe-Coque story

Born from a simple idea, that of reviving abandoned sailboats, Passe-Coque has already saved more than 25 boats from deconstruction for as many supported projects.
A team of experts accompanied by volunteers, patrons or committed partners bring the adventure to life across the world.
