Surprisingly, the swell has dropped. Even more surprising, the forecast had anticipated it.

And of course, we had to clean-up after he was gone..., those guys poop as soon as they land somewhere.
The weather forecast is anticipating some thunderstorms for tonight...
This last night at sea has been quite something... Thunderstorms, thunderclaps, lightnings, the full show! And with the wind we had, we were going faster than any previous day of this leg. As a result, we were early, and we tacked a couple of times in front of the Tiputa Pass, waiting for daylight to enter it.
We got in at 5am, with the sun, the wind and the tide behind us, right by the book!
Some great dolphins are entering the pass with us.
With the current and the wind in the same direction, the pass was as flat as a lake.
We rounded the island visible from outside through the pass, the one with a single palm tree on it. We dropped the anchor in 40 feet of water. And we did see it on the bottom, the water is crystal clear.
All around, it's all blue and green... Blue for the water (all kinds of blues), and green for the palm trees...
Wow! I know, again...
The lagoon is so big that we could not see the other side of the atoll.
